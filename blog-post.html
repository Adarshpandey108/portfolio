Understanding Neural Networks

By Adarsh Pandey - September 19, 2024

What are Neural Networks?

Neural networks are a subset of machine learning models inspired by the structure and functioning of the human brain. They consist of interconnected layers of nodes (neurons), each of which processes inputs to produce an output. These networks can learn from vast amounts of data and have been used in a variety of applications such as image recognition, natural language processing, and predictive modeling.

How Do Neural Networks Work?

Neural networks process data through layers: an input layer, hidden layers, and an output layer. Each connection between the neurons has a weight that is adjusted during training. The learning process involves minimizing the error (loss) between the predicted and actual outputs through an optimization process called backpropagation. Over time, neural networks can learn highly complex patterns in data.

Applications of Neural Networks

Neural networks have revolutionized industries. In healthcare, they are used for diagnosing diseases from medical images. In finance, they help in predicting stock prices and managing risks. In autonomous driving, they enable cars to understand and navigate their environment. These applications show the power and versatility of neural networks in solving real-world problems.
